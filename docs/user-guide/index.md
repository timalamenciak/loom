# Annotator guide

This guide is for people assigned to identify and record causal claims from
scientific articles in Loom.

## Your goal

For each distinct causal claim:

1. Preserve the text that supports the claim.
2. Describe the cause and effect as two nodes.
3. Connect the nodes with a causal edge.
4. Complete the fields shown by the project's active CAMO schema.

Loom preserves the article, excerpts, annotator, schema version, and ontology
snapshot so that the resulting annotation can be reviewed and reproduced.

## The workflow

| Stage | What you do | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Assignment | Open a paper from **My queue** | The assignment becomes **In progress** |
| Grounding | Select supporting text and add it to the excerpt bin | The claim remains tied to its source |
| Article context | Complete **Source doc** once | Shared study context is available to the article's edges |
| Nodes | Describe the cause and effect | At least two reusable nodes exist |
| Edge | Connect the nodes and annotate the causal relationship | A draft edge is saved |
| Completion | Check the edge and mark it done | The edge becomes **Complete** |
| Submission | Submit the finished article | The assignment becomes **Submitted** and read-only |

## Before you begin

You need:

- a Loom account;
- membership in the project; and
- at least one article assigned to you.

If **My queue** is empty, ask a project administrator to assign an article.

## Important habits

- Ground annotations in the article's text whenever the text is available.
- Record only what the article supports. Do not fill gaps by guessing.
- Select ontology results when there is a suitable match. Do not invent
  ontology identifiers.
- Reuse an existing node when it represents the same entity, attribute, and
  direction.
- Treat **Save**, **Mark done**, and **Submit for review** as separate actions.

!!! note "The form can vary by project"
    Loom builds annotation forms from the active CAMO schema. Field names,
    choices, required fields, and section groupings may differ between
    projects or schema versions. Follow the labels and help text displayed in
    your form.
