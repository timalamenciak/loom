# Create edges

An edge records a directed causal relationship between two nodes.

## Before creating an edge

Confirm that:

- the claim is supported by the article;
- the relevant excerpts are in the excerpt bin; and
- the cause and effect nodes already exist.

Loom requires at least two nodes before **Save edge** is available.

## Create a grounded edge

1. Select every excerpt that directly supports the claim.
2. Select **Use for edge**.
3. Confirm the selected passages under **Grounding excerpts**.
4. Choose the cause as the **Subject** node.
5. Choose the effect as the **Object** node.
6. Complete the schema-generated sections.
7. Select **Save edge**.
8. Confirm that the edge appears in the graph panel with a **Draft** badge.

You can use **+ Add edge** to open an empty form. Attach supporting excerpts
before saving whenever source text is available.

## Complete the annotation layers

CAMO organizes complementary information about a causal claim. Depending on
the active schema, the form can ask about:

- the relationship and strength of the claim;
- the causal account or reasoning expressed by the article;
- contextual and mechanistic features; and
- the evidential basis or study design.

Sections may be collapsed. Open each section and review every visible field.
Use the field descriptions and available choices rather than relying on a list
from another project or schema version.

## Handle missing information

Do not guess. When the schema offers **not addressed**, use it only when the
article does not provide the requested information. Leave an optional field
empty when that is the clearer and permitted representation.

If the form rejects a value, read the message beside the field, correct the
entry, and save again. Loom does not save an invalid form submission.

## Check direction

Before saving, read the graph as a sentence:

> Subject → relationship → Object

For the working example, nitrogen addition is the cause and increasing plant
biomass is the effect. Reversing the nodes would change the claim.
